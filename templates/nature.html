Purandar Fort

Positioned 40 km southeast of Pune, Purandar Fort stands at an elevation of 1,355 meters. Steeped in history, it served as a key strategic fortification during various reigns. It offers breathtaking views of the Sahyadri mountain range and the nearby Purandar village. The fort has two distinct hillocks, Purandar and Vajragad (also known as Rudramal). Visitors can explore its ancient bastions, gates, and temples, making it a haven for history buffs and adventure seekers alike. With its rich historical significance dating back to the Yadava dynasty, Purandar Fort has witnessed numerous battles and changing rulers. Its imposing structure and intricate architecture stand as testaments to its glorious past, attracting history enthusiasts and architecture aficionados alike.

Taljai Hill

Situated in the Sahakar Nagar area of Pune, Taljai Hill is a serene escape from the city's hustle and bustle. Despite its modest height of around 300 meters, it boasts lush greenery and diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can trek through its trails and enjoy panoramic views of Pune city and its surroundings. The hill is a popular spot for morning walks, birdwatching, and picnics, offering tranquility amidst nature's beauty.Despite its relatively small size, Taljai Hill offers a tranquil retreat for visitors seeking solace in nature's embrace. The hilltop's panoramic vistas, coupled with the gentle rustle of leaves and melodious chirping of birds, create a serene ambiance ideal for relaxation and rejuvenation..

Tamhini Ghat

Tamhini Ghat, located approximately 50 km from Pune, is a scenic mountain pass in the Western Ghats. It's renowned for its stunning landscapes, including verdant hills, dense forests, and cascading waterfalls, especially during the monsoon season. The ghat road offers thrilling drives with breathtaking views of the valley below. Adventure enthusiasts flock to Tamhini Ghat for activities like hiking, photography, and waterfall rappelling. It's also a favored spot for nature lovers seeking respite from urban life amidst the region's natural splendor.Tamhini Ghat's allure lies not only in its scenic beauty but also in its biodiversity, with a rich variety of flora and fauna adorning its landscape. Its winding roads and hidden waterfalls beckon adventurers and explorers to unravel the secrets of this picturesque region, making it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and thrill-seekers alike.
